I spent 20 years in banking. Then I shipped two software products in the same year — with zero coding background.

No CS degree. No dev bootcamp. No technical co-founder.

Just a problem I wanted to solve, a lot of late nights, and AI tools I had to learn by doing.

---

Here is the honest version of how it went.

I wanted a simple way to track my calories and macros without being overwhelmed by complicated apps that felt built for athletes, not regular people.

So I built one.

Fuel Tracker is a calorie and macro tracking web app — clean, simple, with a database of over 3 million foods including restaurant meals. You can set personalized goals, track your weight over time, and pick up where you left off on any device.

It took me longer than I expected. I made mistakes I could not Google my way out of. And I shipped it anyway.

---

Once I got through that first build, I realized something.

The process I stumbled through — prompting AI the right way, staying unstuck, going from idea to live product — was actually repeatable. I had documented enough of it that I could teach it.

So I packaged everything into a second product: "Ship It Without Code."

It is a $19.99 kit with 50 of the actual AI prompts I used, the real build story (including what failed), and three worksheets to help you go from idea to launched.

Not polished. Not a course. Just the real stuff that worked for a banker with no technical background.

---

Two products. Both live. Both built solo.

I am telling you this not to flex — I am telling you because 18 months ago I would not have believed this was possible for someone like me.

If you have an idea sitting in a notes app somewhere, or you have been waiting until you "know enough" to start — this is me saying: you probably know more than you think.
