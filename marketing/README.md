# Fuel Tracker — Your AI Marketing Agents 🤖

You have **4 AI marketing agents**. Think of each one as a specialist employee
who already knows your product and your voice. You give them a task; they hand
you ready-to-use marketing. **You review and post** (nothing auto-publishes —
you stay in control, $0 cost).

## The team
| Agent | What it does | Ask it for… |
|---|---|---|
| **content-agent** | Social posts & captions | "Give me 7 Instagram posts for this week" |
| **scheduling-agent** | A posting calendar | "Build me a 2-week posting schedule" |
| **email-agent** | Email sequences | "Write my 7-day trial-to-paid email series" |
| **youtube-agent** | Video ideas & scripts | "Give me 10 YouTube ideas + script the best one" |

## How to use them
Just ask in plain English, naming the agent. Examples:
- *"Use the **content-agent** to write 5 TikTok captions about portion sizes."*
- *"Use the **email-agent** to write the welcome series."*
- *"Use the **scheduling-agent** to plan next week using the posts in marketing/content."*
- *"Use the **youtube-agent** to script a 'calorie counting for beginners' video."*

(In Claude Code you can also type `/agents` to see them.)

## The secret sauce: the Brand Brief
All 4 agents read **`marketing/BRAND-BRIEF.md`** before they write — that's why
they sound like you and get the facts right. When something changes (new price,
new feature, your custom domain), **update the Brand Brief once** and every agent
gets smarter automatically.

## Where content gets saved
Generated batches can be saved in **`marketing/content/`** as dated files, so you
build a library you can reuse and repurpose.

## A simple weekly rhythm (free, ~1–2 hrs/week)
1. **Mon:** Ask content-agent for the week's posts → review.
2. **Mon:** Ask scheduling-agent to slot them into a calendar.
3. **Through the week:** post from the drafts (phone, copy/paste).
4. **Once:** ask email-agent to set up your welcome + trial series (set-and-forget).
5. **Weekly:** ask youtube-agent for one video idea + script if you're doing video.

> Start small. Even 3 posts/week, consistently, beats a big launch you can't keep up.
