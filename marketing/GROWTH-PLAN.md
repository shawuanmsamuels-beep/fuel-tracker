# Grow My Presence — A Real Plan
### For Shawuan · Fuel Tracker + Ship It Without Code · Solo · $0 budget

> The honest version. No "go viral" promises — just where to show up, what to post,
> and a rhythm you can actually keep. Built around the channels you already have.

---

## The one rule (everything else is detail)
**Consistency beats intensity. One step at a time.** (Your own kit's lesson.)
A small amount of content posted every week for 90 days will out-perform a giant
burst you can't sustain. Pick a rhythm you can keep on your worst week.

You have **two different buyers** — don't blend them in a single post:
- **Fuel Tracker** → people who want to **track calories / lose weight** (health audience).
- **Ship It Without Code** → people who want to **build & launch a product** (maker audience).

Your **founder story** ("20 years in banking, zero code, shipped 2 products") is the
bridge that makes both interesting. Alternate which product you feature; let the story
carry both.

## The 5 moves that actually grow a presence from zero
1. **Go where people already are.** Don't broadcast into an empty room — post into
   communities and use algorithm platforms (Reels, Shorts, Pinterest search).
2. **Make once, post everywhere.** One idea → a Reel → a Short → a Pin → an FB post →
   a tweet. This is how solo founders look "everywhere" without doing 6x the work.
3. **Value or story first, link second.** Especially in FB Groups / Reddit — give
   something useful or be honest; follow each community's self-promo rules.
4. **Engage 10 min/day.** Reply to every comment early on. Relationships > reach when small.
5. **One compounding engine.** Pick YouTube (recommended) or Pinterest and commit to it
   weekly for 90 days. Compounding only works if you don't quit.

## Your sustainable weekly rhythm (~3–5 hrs total)
- **Sunday — batch session (90 min):**
  - Ask the **content-agent** for the week's posts.
  - Film **3–4 short videos** in one sitting (Reels/Shorts/TikTok use the same clips).
  - Make/queue **5 Pinterest pins**.
  - Save everything to drafts.
- **Mon–Sat — 10–15 min/day:** post the scheduled item, reply to comments.
- **Once a week:** publish **1 YouTube video** (script it with the **youtube-agent**).
- **Day off:** rest. Seriously. Burnout kills more launches than bad content.

## Channel playbooks (the specifics)

### 📸 Instagram (reach engine)
- **Reels > static posts** — Reels get shown to non-followers. Hook in the first 1 second.
- Use trending audio; keep it 15–30s; show the app/screen.
- 5–10 relevant hashtags; CTA in caption; **link in bio** (see Link Hub below).
- Repurpose the same video to TikTok and YouTube Shorts.

### 📘 Facebook (warm sales + community)
- Post your **personal story** to your profile first (your network already trusts you).
- Then share value in **relevant Groups** (weight-loss groups for FT; maker/side-hustle
  groups for the kit) — **follow each group's rules**, lead with help, not links.
- Longer, conversational posts work well here (Posts 3 & 8 are written for this).

### 🎥 YouTube (your long-term engine)
- **Searchable titles** people actually type: "How to count calories for beginners,"
  "I built an app with no coding background."
- **First video = your story** — it sells both products in one go.
- Repurpose your Reels as **Shorts** to feed the channel between long videos.
- Script every video with the **youtube-agent**.

### 📌 Pinterest (evergreen, underrated)
- It's **SEO, not social** — keyword-rich pin **titles + descriptions** matter most.
- Vertical images (1000×1500). Each pin links to a product page.
- Create boards: *Calorie Tracking Tips*, *Macro-Friendly Eating*, *Weight Loss Motivation*
  (→ Fuel Tracker) and *Start a Side Hustle*, *AI Tools for Beginners*, *Build Without Code*
  (→ the kit).
- Pins resurface for **months/years** — this is slow-burn traffic that keeps paying.

### 💼 LinkedIn (founder brand)
- **Native text story posts** perform best — tell the banking→founder journey.
- Put the **link in the first comment**, not the post body (LinkedIn suppresses outbound links).
- 2×/week. Your audience here is professional and receptive to reinvention stories.

### 🐦 X/Twitter (build-in-public journal)
- Low effort. Share progress, numbers, lessons. **Pin your launch thread.**
- Don't expect reach yet — it grows slowly as you show up.

## The 90-day arc
- **Weeks 1–2 — Launch:** FB + IG launch posts; set up YouTube, Pinterest, Link Hub.
- **Weeks 3–6 — Rhythm:** weekly batch; start posting YouTube; pin to Pinterest consistently.
- **Weeks 7–12 — Double down:** check what's working, lean into it; grow the email list;
  let the compounding channels (YouTube/Pinterest) build.

> Start with the warm channels, build the long-term engine, and keep showing up.
